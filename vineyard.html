Our Vineyards

The first planting of Cabernet Sauvignon was in 1979 after John &Jennifer purchased 200 acres in Walla Walla Valley in Washington State. 5 years later, they purchase another 200 acres in the same valley. They have grown the vineyard to be over 1,000 pristine acres within Walla Walla Valley, Columbia Valley, and Red Mountain.

The Washington State region is recognized as one of the world's most exciting and diversified regions for growing wine.

Looking at the image above, you can see that Washington State and the Bordeaux region of France share the same latitude, 48°. Washington State has the added benefit of a longer growing season and cooler nighttime temperatures.
